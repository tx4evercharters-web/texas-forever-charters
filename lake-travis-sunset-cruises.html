The Best Way to End a Day in Austin

There's no shortage of ways to watch a Texas sunset. But watching it drop behind the limestone bluffs of the Hill Country from a boat anchored in Devil's Cove — cold drink in hand, water still warm from the day — is genuinely in a category of its own.

The Lake Travis sunset cruise is our most requested charter, and it earns that every time. The lake faces west across open Hill Country, which means you get the full show: orange turning to red, red bleeding into purple, the sky reflected on the water below.

You don't need a special occasion. Friday evenings, anniversary trips, first dates, and groups of friends who just want a proper Texas evening — they all work perfectly for a sunset cruise.

What to Expect

Sunset cruises typically run 2–3 hours and depart in the late afternoon from Volente Beach Waterpark & Resort. Captain DJ or Dane takes you out on the lake, usually heading toward Devil's Cove, where you'll anchor as the light starts to change.

From there, it's yours. Float on the mats, stay on deck, pour drinks, take photos — no agenda, no itinerary. Your captain handles the timing so you're in the right spot when the sky does what Texas skies do at dusk.

  • 2–3 hour cruise window
  • Late afternoon departure from Volente Beach
  • Anchored at Devil's Cove for the sunset
  • Float mats and swim time included
  • BYOB — bring wine, beer, whatever you like
  • Both boats available for sunset cruises
  • Catering available through Beachside Billies

Make It a Special Evening

Sunset cruises on Lake Travis have become a go-to for proposals, anniversaries, and romantic dates in Austin. The setting does a lot of the work — you just have to show up. If you're planning something specific, let us know when you book and we'll make sure the evening is set up right.

Groups work just as well. A sunset cruise with 10 or 15 friends on the Carver is one of those nights that gets talked about for a long time. BYOB means you control the vibe — bring the right playlist, the right cooler, and the right people.
