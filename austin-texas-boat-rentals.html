Why Captained, Not Self-Drive?

Most boat rentals in Austin hand you the keys and a hopeful smile. You're responsible for navigation, safety, docking, fueling up, and keeping track of everyone on board. If you've never run a boat on a 65-mile lake before, that's a steep learning curve before lunch.

Texas Forever Charters works differently. Every charter comes with a licensed, experienced captain — DJ or Dane — who takes care of everything from departure to return. You don't need a boating license, prior experience, or any knowledge of Lake Travis. Your captain knows every cove, current, and sandbar on the lake.

It's a fundamentally better way to spend a day on the water. You're not renting a boat. You're booking an experience with someone who's been doing this for years and genuinely loves Lake Travis. All you bring is the crew and the cooler.

What You'll See on Lake Travis

Lake Travis has more to offer than most people realize. The main basin is wide and dramatic, but the real highlights are tucked into the coves and inlets along the Hill Country shoreline.

  • Devil's Cove, the most iconic stop on Lake Travis
  • Mansfield Dam, the landmark that created the lake
  • Texas Hill Country limestone bluffs
  • Volente Beach and surrounding coves
  • Open basin cruising and scenic water views
  • Hidden anchoring spots away from the crowds

Most charters include time anchored at Devil's Cove, where guests swim, float on the mats, and take in the views. It's been a Lake Travis staple for decades and never gets old.

What to Expect on Your Charter

Most groups have never been on a private boat charter before, so here's how a typical day actually plays out — from arrival to disembarkation.

Arrival (15 minutes before departure). You'll meet your captain at the Volente Beach Waterpark & Resort marina. Plan to arrive a little early — there's parking on-site, restrooms, and a snack bar if anyone needs to grab last-minute provisions. The waterpark itself is included in your charter for free, before and after, so come early or stay late if you want to enjoy it.

Departure & briefing (10 minutes). Captain DJ or Dane walks the group through the boat, the safety basics, where everything is, and asks where you'd like to go. Most groups have a rough idea — Devil's Cove is the popular pick — but the captain knows alternatives if the cove's crowded or the wind's wrong.

Out on the water (the bulk of your time). Whatever charter length you've booked (2 hours, 4 hours, full day), this is your time. Cruise, anchor, swim, float on the mats, jump off the yacht's platform (yacht charters only), eat, drink, take photos. The captain handles navigation, anchoring, conditions, and getting you the most out of every minute.

Heading back (15 minutes). The captain gives you a 15-minute warning so you can pack up your cooler, dry off, and enjoy the cruise back to Volente. Disembark, tip your captain (20% gratuity, day-of, cash/Zelle/Venmo), and head out.

Total turnaround is just a few minutes longer than the charter window itself. No fuel stops, no hassle, no figuring anything out.
