# Texas Forever Charters — Marketing Angles & Verified Facts

Last updated: 2026-05-12

## Sightseeing Waypoints (verified, use freely)

### Villa Del Lago
- 15,400 sq ft Mediterranean-style estate
- South shore of Lake Travis, 12400 Cedar Street
- 20+ hillside acres (originally listed as 26 acres)
- Built 2004 by Austin-based Sendero Homes
- Distinctive look: red-tiled roof, white stucco walls — Mykonos-in-Hill-Country feel
- Listed 2022 at $45M — Texas's most expensive home on the market at the time
- Reduced to $35M in 2023
- Built by Lan-Feng and Benjamin Wu; sold to a Houston LLC (12400 Cedar-Land LLC) in 2018
- Marketing copy options:
  - "Villa Del Lago — the 15,400 sq ft Mediterranean estate that was listed as Texas's most expensive home at $45 million"
  - "One of the most photographed mansions in Austin"
  - "Built on a 20-acre hillside with views over Lake Travis"
- AVOID: "Largest house in Austin" (not verifiable)
- AVOID: Current asking price (changes, becomes outdated)
- Operational note: South shore. Verify line-of-sight from north-shore tour routes before featuring on every tour.

### The Oasis
- Established 1982 by Houston entrepreneur Beau Theriot
- Self-proclaimed "Sunset Capital of Texas"
- Sits on a bluff 450 feet above Lake Travis
- 30,000 sq ft — largest outdoor restaurant in Texas
- In 2019 it was the 4th largest restaurant in the world by seating capacity
- Seats up to 2,500-2,800 across 40+ outdoor decks
- Started as a 500-acre ranch Theriot bought in 1979
- Originally opened as "The Oasis Restaurant and Cantina Del Lago"
- June 2005: lightning strike caused major fire; reopened 2009 with more seating
- Tradition: bell rung at sunset every evening
- Address: 6550 Comanche Trail, Austin, TX
- Marketing copy options:
  - "The Oasis — most visitors see it from the cliff above. From the water, you see what they can't: the 450-foot bluff it sits on, the multi-tier architecture, and one of the largest restaurants in the world from below."
  - "The Sunset Capital of Texas — and the sunsets they're famous for? You're right in them on a sunset charter."
  - "A view of The Oasis no other tour shows you — from underneath the bluff, looking up."
- Use heavily for sunset cruise marketing — direct alignment.

### Mansfield Dam
- Originally Marshall Ford Dam (1937–1941)
- Renamed Mansfield Dam in 1941 after U.S. Representative J.J. Mansfield
- Full completion: May 1942
- 13 miles northwest of downtown Austin
- 266 feet high (~26 stories), 7,098 feet long
- One of the largest dams in Texas
- Can store 256 billion gallons of floodwater
- Built jointly by Lower Colorado River Authority and U.S. Bureau of Reclamation
- Operated today by the U.S. Army Corps of Engineers
- Lake Travis = only one of the seven Highland Lakes designed specifically for flood control
- Built after catastrophic 1935 flood killed dozens and caused millions in damage
- Then-Senator Lyndon B. Johnson was instrumental in securing federal funding
- LBJ later said: "Of all the endeavors on which I have worked in public life, I am proudest of the accomplishment in developing the Colorado River."
- First hydroelectric power generation began January 27, 1941
- Marketing copy options:
  - "Mansfield Dam — the 26-story concrete wall that holds back 256 billion gallons of floodwater and created Lake Travis itself in 1942."
  - "Originally Marshall Ford Dam. Renamed after Representative J.J. Mansfield, who fought for years to get it built. Lyndon Johnson, then a young senator, secured the federal funding."
  - "Lake Travis exists because of a catastrophic 1935 flood that killed dozens. The dam was the answer."
- Use heavily for tours featuring scale/history. Strong for corporate/educational charters.

### Lake Travis (general context)
- 63.5 miles long (some sources cite 65) — largest of seven Highland Lakes
- Named after William Barret Travis, commander at the Alamo
- 270+ miles of shoreline at full capacity
- Water levels swing 50+ feet between wet/dry periods due to flood-control purpose
- Use this for destination-significance framing without overpromising lake traversal

  Kilpatrick (no rotating crew, no contractors)

  charter customers

- "Coast Guard certified" or any federal merchant marine credential
- Any safety certification not actually held

## Voice Decisions

### What to lean into
- Premium yacht charter (not "boat rental")
- Owner-operated, captain-led personal brand
- Honest about what you offer: a curated multi-cove experience in a beautiful section of the lake
- Volente Beach Waterpark integration (genuinely unique)
- Small groups, families, couples, scenic experiences
- Texas Hill Country aesthetic
- "Your safety and pleasure are our top priority"

### What to avoid
- Don't claim full lake traversal (~65 miles) — operationally unrealistic
- Don't market as a party-tier operator — the bachelor/bachelorette segment is owned by KAW and Lake Travis Yacht Rentals
- Don't mention Hippie Hollow (brand mismatch with premium positioning)
- Don't name specific celebrity-home owners (privacy, accuracy decay, most Austin celebrities are on Lake Austin not Lake Travis)
- Don't oversell the Carver as a tireless yacht — she's a beautifully maintained 30-year-old classic
- Don't claim "largest house in Austin" for Villa Del Lago (not verifiable)

### Operational reality the marketing should respect
- Carver is a 1996 Aft Cabin — needs care, not punishment. Marketing should position as classic/curated, not workhorse.
- Routes radiate from Volente Beach (north shore). Long-distance trips aren't the product.
- Charter sweet spot is 4-5 hours. Past 5 hours, customers tire.
